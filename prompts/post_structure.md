# Post Structure — Build the post in this exact order

Every LinkedIn post MUST follow this 5-part structure. Build each section deliberately, in order. Do not skip or merge sections.

---

## Part 1: HOOK (first 1-2 lines)

The hook is the most important part of the post. It determines whether people click "see more."

**Rules:**
- First 7 words must create a pattern interrupt — break the reader's scroll
- NEVER start with "I"
- Must be specific, not generic — a real situation, number, or observation
- Must create a curiosity gap — the reader needs to know what comes next
- Must promise a payoff — the reader senses they'll learn something or be entertained
- Keep it to 1-2 short lines maximum

**Hook formulas that work:**
- Scene-setting: "Last Tuesday's partner call went off the rails." (drops into a moment)
- Counterintuitive: "The worst onboarding we ever did became our best case study."
- Number-led: "47 partner calls in 3 weeks taught me one thing."
- Challenge: "Most partner managers get this completely wrong."
- Confession: "Took me 6 months to realize our integration was broken."

**Hook patterns that are BANNED:**
- Starting with "I" (any form)
- "In today's..." / "In the world of..."
- "Have you ever wondered..."
- "Let me tell you about..."
- "Here's why..." (without preceding context)
- Anything that could be a brand account's first line

---

## Part 2: TENSION / SETUP (2-4 short paragraphs)

This is where you build the story. The reader is hooked — now give them the context.

**Rules:**
- Very short paragraphs: 1-2 lines each. Never a block of text.
- Build tension or surprise — something unexpected, difficult, or counterintuitive
- Be specific: names (if appropriate), numbers, real details
- Show, don't tell: "The spreadsheet had 200 rows of partner data and zero logic" beats "We had a data problem"
- One idea per paragraph
- This section should make the reader think "what happened next?" or "I've been there"

**Tone:**
- Conversational — like you're telling a colleague over coffee
- Honest about difficulty — "we had no idea" is better than "we strategically pivoted"
- Light humour when natural — dry, self-aware, never forced

---

## Part 3: INSIGHT / TURN (1-2 paragraphs)

The "aha" moment. What you learned, realized, or figured out.

**Rules:**
- This is the value of the post — the reason someone should care
- Must be a genuine insight, not a platitude
- Be concrete: "We started sending a 2-minute Loom before every kickoff call" beats "Communication is key"
- Can be surprising or counterintuitive — those perform best
- Don't over-explain — trust the reader

**Test:** If the insight could apply to literally any company in any industry, it's too generic. Rewrite.

---

## Part 4: RESOLUTION / RESULT (1 paragraph)

What happened as a result. Close the story loop.

**Rules:**
- Short — 1-2 lines max
- Concrete result when possible: "Partner activation went from 3 weeks to 4 days"
- Can be humble: "Still not perfect, but we're getting there"
- Don't oversell: "it worked" is fine — no need for "it was a game-changer"
- This paragraph should feel like a satisfying ending to the story

---

## Part 5: CTA + HASHTAGS (last 2-3 lines)

End with engagement. The CTA exists to generate comments, not likes.

**CTA rules:**
- Must be a specific open question directly related to the post's story
- The question must invite a personal response — something the reader has their own answer to
- NEVER generic: "What do you think?", "Agree?", "Thoughts?"
- NEVER engagement bait: "Comment YES if...", "Like if you relate"
- Good pattern: "What's your [specific thing related to the post]?"
- Leave a blank line before the CTA

**CTA examples that work:**
- "What's the weirdest partner request you've had to say yes to?"
- "How do you handle the first 48 hours after a partner signs?"
- "What's one integration that looked simple but turned into a 3-month project?"

**Hashtag rules:**
- 1-3 hashtags, placed on the last line after the CTA
- Niche > generic: #HRtech > #Business
- Separated from CTA by a blank line

---

## Full post skeleton

```
[HOOK — 1-2 lines, pattern interrupt, no "I"]

[TENSION — 2-4 short paragraphs, build the story]

[INSIGHT — 1-2 paragraphs, the "aha" moment]

[RESOLUTION — 1 paragraph, close the loop]

[CTA — specific question that invites personal responses]

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2
```

## Word count

The complete post (all 5 parts) must be 150-220 words. Budget roughly:
- Hook: 10-20 words
- Tension: 60-90 words
- Insight: 30-50 words
- Resolution: 15-30 words
- CTA + hashtags: 15-25 words
